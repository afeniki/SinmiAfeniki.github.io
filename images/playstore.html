How can app developers make their products more popular in Google Play Store?

With around three million apps available on Google Play Store, developing apps that stand out amongst the competition poses a challenge for app developers. To differentiate themselves in this oversaturated market, they need to pinpoint essential factors that play a role in customers’ decision-making process.

The objective of this project is to deliver insights to understand customer demands better and thus help developers to popularize the product. To clarify, the ‘popular’ in this project means a high number of installations.

Data Understanding

The dataset for Google App Store analysis is from Kaggle. It has 10841 observations(number of apps) and 13 variables(number of characteristics for each app). The target variable is “Installs” (number of installation), and explanatory variables include category, price, ratings, and reviews, etc. Specifically, ‘Rating’ is a continuous variable with a scale of 1–5.

Data Cleaning

The raw data contains too many categorical variables and about 500 missing values in the rating column. Data cleaning includes the following steps:

  1. We dropped all the observations with missing values. Although it means a loss of 5% data, it’s hard to impute the Review score with existing data. If individual reviews for each app are available, the imputation is possible through the text mining approach — predicting the missing rating using an inverse regression model.
  2. We dropped‘app names,’ ‘Current Ver,’ ‘Android Ver,’ and ‘Genres’ columns. Because the information is not so useful in this project and ‘Genres’ is also a subset of the ‘Category’ column.
  3. We Converted the ‘Last Updated’ column into the number of days since the last update, which was calculated by subtracting it from the date when compiling the dataset.
